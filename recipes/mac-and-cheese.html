Creamy Baked Mac and Cheese

I found this recipe online a few years ago and made some adjustments to suit my taste. It's definitely fattening, but insanely satisfying and perfect for a holiday meal or get-together.

Ingredients

  • Cavatappi pasta (1lb / one box)
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup all purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 cups whole milk
  • 2 1/2 cups half and half
  • 4 cups freshly-shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1 1/2 cups freshly-shredded gruyere cheese
  • 1/2 cup freshly-shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 tbsp. salt
  • 1/2 tsp. black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp. paprika

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 325 degrees and grease a 9x13" baking dish.
  2. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil, and cook the cavatappi 1 minute less than the package directs for al dente. Drain and drizzle with a small amount of olive oil to prevent sticking.
  3. While the water is coming to a boil, shred the cheeses and mix together. Measure out 3 cups for the sauce, and split the remaining amount for the layers in the dish (more on that later).
  4. Melt butter in a large saucepan over medium heat. Sprinkle in the flour and whisk together. Cook for one minute, whisking often. Slowly pour in your whole milk and half-and-half while whisking, until smooth.
  5. Continue to heat and whisk very often until thickened to a very thick consistency. You should feel resistance when whisking.
  6. Remove from the heat and add in your spices and 1 1/2 cups of the cheeses, stirring until melted and combined. Do the same for another 1 1/2 cups of cheese.
  7. Add the drained pasta to your sauce, stirring to combine. Pour half of the pasta mixture into the baking dish, covering with 1 1/2 cups of cheese. Add the rest of the pasta and cover with the last 1 1/2 cups of cheese.
  8. Bake for 15-20 minutes, broiling for the last minute or so to get a nice golden crisp on the top.
